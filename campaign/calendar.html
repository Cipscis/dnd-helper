The calendar is made up of 13 months, each consisting of 28 days. As such, a whole year takes 364 days. The days of each month line up with the phases of the moon, such that the 1st day of every month is a new moon and the 15th is a full moon. The start of a new year is marked by the first day of spring.

Each day of the week is named after one of the gods:

  1. Ara's day
  2. Tyro's day
  3. Ilune's day
  4. Burke's day
  5. Wan's day
  6. Nook's day
  7. Thresher's day

In many cultures and professions, Ara's day and Tyro's day are days of rest.
